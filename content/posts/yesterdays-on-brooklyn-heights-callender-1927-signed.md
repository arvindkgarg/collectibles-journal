## Frequently Asked Questions

### What does 'Limited Edition' mean for a book?
A 'Limited Edition' signifies that only a finite number of copies were printed, making them scarcer than standard print runs. When combined with a signature and specific numbering, it makes the individual copy more unique and desirable to collectors.

### What is 'flexible black cloth' binding?
'Flexible black cloth,' also known as limp cloth, refers to a book binding that lacks rigid board covers, allowing the book to bend more easily. This style was often used for certain editions, providing a distinct tactile and aesthetic quality.

### What are 'top edge gilt' and 'deckled edges'?
'Top edge gilt' (t.e.g.) means the top edge of the book's pages has been treated with a thin layer of gold leaf, protecting the pages from dust and adding an elegant finish. 'Deckled edges' refer to intentionally untrimmed, rough-cut page edges, designed to give the book an artisanal or antique appearance.
